# Hype Pencil Prompt Pack

## Purpose

Provide high-signal prompts for exploring Hype design directions in Pencil before converging in Figma.

Use these prompts to generate concepts, not final production UI.

## How to use this pack

Recommended workflow:

1. generate 3 to 5 directions in Pencil
2. compare them against `design_direction_brief.md`
3. choose one direction
4. rebuild and systematize it in Figma

Do not accept a Pencil result just because it looks polished.

Judge it against:

- Sarajevo feel
- mood-first discovery
- concierge energy
- trust and local specificity

## Global prompt rules

Use these instructions in every Pencil prompt:

- mobile-first app UI
- iOS and Android product feel
- bilingual Sarajevo city discovery app
- warm editorial style
- avoid generic startup app look
- avoid purple SaaS gradients
- avoid overusing identical cards
- emphasize mood, nightlife, culture, and hidden gems
- the app should feel premium, local, and trustworthy

## Prompt 1. Core Home Direction

Use when:

- you want the main flagship concept

Prompt:

```text
Design a mobile app home screen for Hype, a Sarajevo-first bilingual city discovery app described as “the digital pulse of the city.” The app helps locals and tourists discover what to do tonight through mood-based recommendations, live events, hidden gems, culture, nightlife, and curated venue picks. The design should feel like a stylish local concierge, not a generic event app or tourist brochure. Use a warm editorial visual language with deep night blue, warm ivory, brass accents, muted terracotta, and subtle texture. Emphasize a strong city-pulse hero, expressive mood shortcuts, a “Tonight” section, and one editorial recommendation block. Avoid repetitive card grids and avoid sterile SaaS UI patterns.
```

## Prompt 2. Warmer Editorial Direction

Use when:

- you want something more atmospheric and premium

Prompt:

```text
Create a high-end mobile app interface for Hype, a Sarajevo nightlife and culture discovery app. The interface should feel like an editorial city guide mixed with a luxury concierge, with emotional mood-based discovery and strong local character. Prioritize typography, warmth, and atmosphere over dense utility grids. Include a mood-led hero, curated recommendations, subtle trust labels, and elegant content blocks for events, venues, and specials. Make it feel rooted in Sarajevo’s evening energy, cafes, jazz, bars, festivals, and hidden courtyards. Avoid looking like a travel booking app, food delivery app, or generic nightlife marketplace.
```

## Prompt 3. Tourist-Utility Direction

Use when:

- you want to test whether the tourist angle can stay elegant

Prompt:

```text
Design a mobile home screen for Hype, a Sarajevo city guide app for both tourists and locals. The design should balance utility and taste: tourists should quickly understand what is happening tonight, where to go, what is worth seeing, and how to navigate the city, while locals should still feel the app is stylish and relevant. Include tourist mode cues, must-see guidance, hidden gems, and tonight recommendations, but keep the interface premium, modern, and culturally grounded. Warm, elegant palette. Clear visual hierarchy. Avoid obvious “tourist app” clichés.
```

## Prompt 4. Tonight-First Direction

Use when:

- you want to sharpen the real-time promise

Prompt:

```text
Design a mobile app screen for “Tonight in Sarajevo” inside Hype, a live city pulse app. The goal is to answer the question “What’s happening tonight?” in under 10 seconds. Show mood-based suggestions, live events, great bars, cultural picks, and one clear featured recommendation. The UI should feel alive, local, and trustworthy. Use compact time-first rows mixed with one or two richer editorial cards. Warm urban palette, premium typography, subtle motion cues, and strong emphasis on quick decision-making. Avoid endless identical cards or cluttered filters.
```

## Prompt 5. Venue Detail Direction

Use when:

- you want a second screen to validate the system

Prompt:

```text
Design a venue detail screen for Hype, a stylish Sarajevo city discovery app. The venue page should feel trusted, rich, and editorial. Show venue identity, mood, opening hours, insider tips, tonight’s events, daily specials, and actions like directions, website, Instagram, and save. The page should feel like a local recommendation with depth, not just a generic place detail screen. Use warm colors, expressive typography, a strong hero image area, subtle trust markers, and section rhythm that avoids bland stacked cards.
```

## Prompt 6. Explore Direction

Use when:

- you want a browsing/search concept without losing the thesis

Prompt:

```text
Design an Explore screen for Hype, a mood-based Sarajevo discovery app. Users should be able to browse venues, events, and daily specials by mood, category, and neighborhood, but the screen should still feel curated and elegant. Use expressive mood chips, a refined search input, a category system with local personality, and multiple content presentation patterns instead of one repetitive card list. Warm editorial aesthetic, bilingual-ready structure, strong clarity, and premium local feel.
```

## What to evaluate after generation

For each concept, score it mentally on:

1. does it feel like Sarajevo rather than a generic city app
2. does it feel like a concierge rather than a database
3. does it make mood central
4. does it support the “what’s happening tonight” promise
5. does it look credible for both investor pitch and real product

## What to move into Figma

Once a direction wins, rebuild only these first:

1. color system
2. typography system
3. spacing scale
4. mood chip patterns
5. home hero
6. tonight row pattern
7. feature card pattern
8. venue tile pattern

That becomes the seed of the real design system.
